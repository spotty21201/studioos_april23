# AIM Team Structure

This document defines the current team structure of AIM and expands the role of each person in a way that is useful for internal alignment, onboarding, collaboration, and future system documentation.

The descriptions below are written to clarify functional responsibility, likely contribution areas, and the internal logic of how the studio operates. They are not meant to rigidly limit anyone’s role, but to make the studio legible.

---

## 1. Leadership

### Farid Ramdani — Director
Farid Ramdani serves as one of AIM’s Directors and should be understood as part of the studio’s senior decision-making layer. This role carries responsibility for helping steer the direction of the practice at the organizational and business level.

Key responsibilities may include:
- contributing to the overall direction and positioning of the studio
- participating in strategic decision-making across projects, partnerships, and growth
- representing the studio in client, stakeholder, or institutional settings when needed
- helping maintain quality, credibility, and continuity in how the studio operates
- supporting major decisions related to priorities, resourcing, and external relationships

In practical terms, the Director role should be understood as part leadership, part stewardship, and part high-level oversight.

### Dodi Supriyatna — Director
Dodi Supriyatna serves as one of AIM’s Directors and forms part of the studio’s top leadership structure. The role should be understood as both managerial and strategic, with responsibility for helping ensure that the studio remains aligned, effective, and professionally grounded.

Key responsibilities may include:
- helping guide the studio’s direction and major internal decisions
- supporting project positioning, client confidence, and professional credibility
- contributing to business judgment, organizational priorities, and studio stability
- helping maintain alignment between leadership intent and studio execution
- participating in high-level review, problem-solving, and critical decision points

This role should be read as one of senior leadership, continuity, and organizational judgment.

### Doddy Samiaji — Principal
Doddy Samiaji serves as Principal and should be understood as the central strategic and intellectual lead of the studio. This role is not only managerial but also directional: shaping the studio’s thinking, project framing, design logic, client communication, and broader opportunities.

Key responsibilities may include:
- defining the strategic direction of projects and the studio’s value proposition
- leading high-level thinking, concept development, and problem framing
- connecting design work with broader business, development, planning, or decision-making outcomes
- engaging with senior clients, partners, and stakeholders on matters of direction and opportunity
- guiding project narratives, quality expectations, and major review moments
- identifying new opportunities, collaborations, and future growth pathways
- ensuring that AIM’s work remains intellectually strong, commercially aware, and relevant

The Principal role should be understood as the studio’s leading source of direction, synthesis, and judgment.

## 2. Senior Professional Layer

### Ria Asyurani — Senior Associate
Ria Asyurani serves as Senior Associate and should be understood as a key bridge between leadership direction and studio execution. This role is senior enough to carry responsibility not only for production quality, but also for coordination, translation of intent, and day-to-day project coherence.

Key responsibilities may include:
- translating leadership direction into clear project action and team guidance
- supporting project management, design development, and internal coordination
- helping maintain quality control across outputs, documentation, and studio deliverables
- mentoring or guiding more junior studio members where needed
- helping ensure continuity between concept, production, and final project output
- acting as a dependable internal anchor in moving work forward

The Senior Associate role should be understood as a senior execution and coordination role with responsibility for quality, continuity, and delivery discipline.

- helping manage internal records, coordination, and project-related paperwork

This role should be read as a crucial support function that helps transform studio activity into a more organized and sustainable practice.
